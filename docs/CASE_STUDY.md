# Waitily Case Study

## Problem

Many buyers know what they want to buy but do not want to purchase immediately at the current price. Their intent is scattered across wishlists, browser tabs, price trackers, chats and manual reminders.

For retailers and partners, this creates a missed signal: many users may be waiting for the same product, but that demand is invisible and hard to negotiate around.

## Solution

Waitily turns individual purchase intent into aggregated demand pools.

A user submits what they want to buy, their reference price and an optional product URL. Waitily normalizes the request, groups similar demand and opens a time-limited pool. If enough demand appears, the platform can evaluate negotiated outcomes, retailer incentives, cashback options or transparent fallback alternatives.
